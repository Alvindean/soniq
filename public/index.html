About Dr. Dixon

From the Street
to the Archive

Anthony E. Dixon, PhD grew up in Fort Valley, Georgia with every advantage — and still found himself walking from a university graduation ceremony into a Florida state prison. Over five and a half years of incarceration, faith and hard reflection transformed him.

That transformation took him to Indiana University, where he earned a doctorate in African Diaspora History. Today he is a public historian, archivist, keynote speaker, and the founder of Archival and Historical Research Associates — an institution dedicated to preserving and amplifying the Black experience in America.

"Incarceration was a nightmare, a blessing, and a learning experience all rolled into one."

The Black
Seminole
Project

From 1817 to 1858, the United States government fought the Seminole Nation in three wars. The second was not simply about land. It was about slavery — and the Black Seminoles who fought the U.S. military to a costly standstill.

Dr. Dixon is developing a documentary and feature film based on his book Florida's Negro War, bringing the story of America's most successful slave rebellion to the screen for the first time.
